Green Erasmus Workshop

Embedding sustainability into youth work and Erasmus+ projects.

🌿
Date: 2024
Format: Online
Type: Workshop
Participants: Youth workers & volunteers
Open to: All Europe

As part of our commitment to Green Erasmus principles, we organised an interactive online workshop for youth workers, Erasmus+ practitioners, and volunteers interested in making their projects more environmentally sustainable.

Why Green Erasmus?

The European Commission's Green Erasmus initiative encourages all Erasmus+ projects to reduce their environmental footprint. This means choosing low-carbon travel options, reducing waste, incorporating environmental education, and tracking the carbon impact of activities.

This workshop was designed to make these concepts practical, accessible, and actionable — not just theory, but real tools participants could use in their own work immediately.

Workshop Topics

  • What is Green Erasmus and why it matters
  • Calculating and offsetting travel carbon footprint
  • Eco-friendly event planning for youth activities
  • Integrating environmental themes into non-formal education
  • Green reporting in Erasmus+ final reports
  • Sharing tools and resources for sustainable youth work

Outcomes

Participants left with a practical toolkit of resources, a better understanding of EU sustainability requirements, and concrete ideas for embedding green practices into their own projects. Several participants implemented changes in their upcoming Erasmus+ applications as a direct result.

"I had no idea how easy it was to calculate and communicate our project's carbon footprint. This workshop made it feel manageable." — Youth Worker, Germany
