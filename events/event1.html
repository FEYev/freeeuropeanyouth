Erasmus+ Going Virtual - Online Methods for Youth Workers

An Erasmus+ international training course exploring the digital world together.

Date: 2024
Location: Rödental/Germany
Type: Erasmus+ KA153
Participants: 34 youth worker
Countries: 5 E+ program (Slovenia, Poland, Turkiye, Germany, Norway) and 3 EECA partner countries (Georgia, Ukraine, Azerbaijan)

The "Digital Futures" youth exchange brought together 30 young people from five European countries for a 7-day intensive programme focused on digital literacy, online safety, and how technology can be used as a tool for positive social change.

About the Event

The training course “Erasmus+ Going Virtual – Online Methods for Youth Workers” focuses on developing innovative digital youth work practices, particularly for supporting young people at risk of social exclusion during crisis situations. The project brings together youth workers from different countries to explore practical online methods that create safe spaces for young people to share experiences and express their emotions.

Participants learn about modern digital youth work tools, strengthen their skills in promoting social inclusion, and exchange good practices for working with vulnerable youth groups. The training also encourages intercultural dialogue between participants from EU and partner countries while supporting self-reflection on the needs of disadvantaged young people.

Activities & Highlights

  • Digital storytelling workshop — participants created short videos about their communities
  • Online safety simulation — exploring misinformation, privacy, and digital rights
  • Social media for activism — how to use platforms for positive change
  • Intercultural evenings — each country shared food, music, and traditions
  • Closing presentation — groups presented their digital projects to local youth workers

Impact & Outcomes

Erasmus+ Going Virtual – Online Methods for Youth Workers training course had a significant impact on the participants, who were youth workers dealing with vulnerable young individuals. They acquired a valuable digital youth work methodology that could be applied in their future projects, particularly during times of crisis and limited face-to-face communication. Through this training, the participants enhanced their qualifications and learned a range of creative youth work methods that they could use in their daily work with their target groups. The project also gave participants the opportunity to exchange knowledge and experiences with peers from Program and EECA countries, helping them better understand solutions that were successful in other contexts. In addition, they improved their intercultural communication skills and strengthened their capacity to participate in future international activities. As part of the results, the project produced online methods, articles on working with vulnerable youth online, a Toolbox on Google Drive, the Erasmus+ Going Virtual Framework, and an educational video. The project also achieved strong dissemination through local activities, online and offline promotion, and the active involvement of 34 youth workers who now act as ambassadors of the project and its results.

"This was the first time I felt truly at home in a group of people from different countries. The digital workshops changed how I think about my phone and social media." — Oliwia, Poland
